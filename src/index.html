Maynard Ferguson Tribute

Maynard Ferguson's performance in Manchester, England 1960’s

  • 1928: Walter Maynard Ferguson was born in Montreal, Canada.

  • 1937: Began playing the trumpet after hearing the trumpet in church

  • 1939: Age 11 Maynards first major performance soloing with the Canadian Broadcasting Company Orchestra

  • 1945: At 17 Down Beat magazine described him as “A new star on the horizon.”

  • 1948: Immigrated from Canada to the United States to perform with the Stan Kenton Band.

  • 1950: After the Kenton Band Maynard was contracted by Paramount Pictures at first-call trumpet player. He recorded the soundtracks for many movies including Cecil B. De Mille’s The Ten Commandments in 1955.

  • 1956: Maynard accepted an invitation from Morris Levy to lead the Birdland Dream Band in New York City.

  • 1967: After a long stay in NYC leading the Birdland Dream Band the band broke up. Maynard was offered a gig soon after to front an all–star Anglo-American jazz orchestra on a tour of England.

  • 1971: Maynard returned to NYC with his British band for a tour and was recieved with open arms.

  • 1974: Maynard took his band into the recording studio to record the Chameleon album for CBS/Columbia.

  • 1975: Maynard took his band into the recording studio again to record the Primal Scream album for Columbia Records.

  • 1976: One of Maynard’s proudest moments came when he was asked to perform at the closing ceremonies of the 1976 Montreal Olympics.

  • 1977: The track “Gonna Fly Now,” was released as a single and invaded the billboard pop charts. "Gonna Fly Now," would go to be featured in the motion picture "Rocky" staring Sylvester Stallone.

  • Maynard focused heavily on educaiton. During his life he was advocate for jazz education.

  • 2006: Maynard passes away in his home with family and friends.
